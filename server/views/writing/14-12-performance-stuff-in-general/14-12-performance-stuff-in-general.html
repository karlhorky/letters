--- layout: post title: Performance Stuff in General description: What can we do to make pages fast? tags: [css, conf, oakland, performance] --- [@benschwarz](https://twitter.com/benschwarz), Ben Schwarz. [Slides](https://speakerdeck.com/benschwarz/useful-performance-metrics) On the internet, time is money. Queue the stats! 47% of consumers expect a web page to load in two seconds or less. "After a second of so, the user's flow and engagement with the initiated task is broken." -Ilya Grigorik. ## Everyone on your team is responsible for the speed of your site. Perceived speed is on the front-end. _Speed is perceived._ So what can we do to make pages fast? Minify. Use AJAX. Base-64 things. Animate using CSS. Use requestAnimationFrame for JS-based animation. Use video rather than gifs. :( ```position:fixed``` can cause uncessary paints while scrolling. (So can a lot of other things.) ```box-shadow``` can be kiiiinnnd of evil. Make animations small. tl;dr: ## Only load what you need. Inlining critical styles will give you big boosts in speed. _So what else do I need to know?_ 1. *Set a budget.* How much is too much? If you set budgets for images, you can have interesting talks with your designers. (Designers like constraints.) 2. *Use real-time analytics.* GA has the average page load time: this is what will give you data on using CDNs, etc. 3. *Do your own metrics.* ```window.performance.timing``` is a thing you can use in browsers and show you how long it took everything to load. If your site goes beyond a certain number, you can get alerts. You don't even need to fight to use GA or anything else; you have access to these metrics yourself. ```window.performance.getEntries()``` ```window.performance.mark("foo")``` set marks and get them and see how long they took to load. (Realize how slow Disqus is on your site!) [Pingdom](https://www.pingdom.com/), [New Relic](http://newrelic.com/), [CSSStats](http://cssstats.com/), [CalibreApp](http://calibreapp.com/) _Don't rely on what you think you know. Prove it._
